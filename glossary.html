  • A

    1. Accelerometer:

      A device that measures acceleration forces in a specific direction.

    2. Active Braking:

      AKA Damping Light. A feature in BLHeli that improve throttle responsiveness.

    3. Aerial:

      Existing, happening, or operating in the air.

    4. Altitude Hold:

      A flight mode that maintain height, it uses “BARO” sensor.

    5. Ailerons:

      Also known as roll.

    6. Auto Levelling:

      A flight mode that allows the copter to stay level. It uses “ACC” sensor

    7. Altitude:

      A device that measures acceleration forces in a specific direction.

    8. Ardu Pilot Mega:

      A type of flight controller board.

    9. Arducopter:

      A flight controller firmware software.

    10. Almost Ready to Fly:

      An ARTF aircraft package usually includes everything you need to fly.

    11. All up weight:

      Max weight of a copter including LiPo Battery and other parts, ready to fly.
  • B

    1. Barometric Altimeter:

      It measures altitude by using barometric pressure

    2. Battery Eliminator Circuit:

      A voltage regulator found in some ESCs. Designed to provide constant 5V voltage for RC equipment such as Flight controller and radio receiver

    3. Bind and Fly:

      A BNF plane, helicopter, or quadcopter requires only one thing to fly: a BNF transmitter with enough channels.

    4. Bootloader:

      Special code stored in non-volatile memory in a microprocessor that can interface with a PC to download a user’s program.
  • C

    1. CopterControl 3D:

      A type of flight controller.

    2. Center of Gravity:

      The average location of the weight of a copter.

    3. Crius:

      A type of flight controller board.
  • D

    1. Direct Drive:

      A type of prop designed to go straight on a motor shaft without a gear reduction.

    2. Degree of Freedom:

      It give clues as to whats on the “IMU”. For example 6DOF = 3 axis Gyroscope (gyro) + 3 axis acc; 9DOF = 3 axis Gyroscope + 3 axis Accelerometer + 3 axis Magnetometer; 10DOF = same as 9DOF and adds a Barometric Altimeter.

    3. Drone:

      An unmanned aircraft that is guided remotely by a pilot. For military use, these aircraft have weapons and/or surveillance gear. For personal/recreational use, these aircraft often have HD cameras. The term is popular in the media and can be misleading sometimes.

    4. DSM / DSM2 / DSMX:

      These are proprietary technology of an RC equipment maker, Spectrum, and DSM stands for “Digital Spectrum Modulation”.

    5. Direct-Sequence Spread Spectrum:

      Spectrum DSM uses Direct-Sequence Spread Spectrum (DSSS) modulation technique. As with other spread spectrum technologies, the transmitted signal takes up more bandwidth than the information signal that modulates the carrier or broadcast frequency. The name ‘spread spectrum’ comes from the fact that the carrier signals occur over the full bandwidth (spectrum) of a device’s transmitting frequency.
  • E

    1. Electonically Erasable Programmable Read Only Memory:

      A type of non-volatile memory used in computers and other electronic devices to store small amounts of data that must be saved when power is removed, e.g.,static calibration/reference tables. Unlike bytes in most other kinds of non-volatile memory, individual bytes in a traditional EEPROM can be independently read, erased, and re-written.

    2. Elevator:

      Also known as pitch.

    3. Electromagnetic Interference:

      EMI is disturbance that affects an electrical circuit due to either electromagnetic induction or electromagnetic radiation emitted from an external source.

    4. Electronic Speed Control:

      The ESC is used to convert signal from Flight controller or radio receiver, and apply the right current to the electric motors. It also usually includes a Battery Elimination Circuit (BEC), which provides power for the RC system and other onboard electronics.
  • F

    1. Federal Aviation Administration:

      An United States Department of Transportation Agency, it has authority to regulate and oversee all aspects of American civil aviation.

    2. Flight Controller:

      Flight Controller is the brain of your multirotor.

    3. Field-effect transistor:

      FET is a device which enables us to use one smaller electrical signal to control another bigger one.

    4. Field of View:

      A measurement how much environment you can see through a camera lens. Usually measured in degrees.

    5. First Person View:

      A technique that uses an onboard FPV camera and wireless connection, to allow a pilot on the ground to see a live stream video while flying, throught a FPV goggle or monitor.

    6. Future Technology Devices International:

      The name of the company that makes the chips. A standard to convert USB to serial communications. Available as a chip for boards that have a USB connector, or in a cable to connected to breakout pins.
  • G

    1. Ground Control Station:

      Software used to monitor a Flight Controller Board. The OpenPilot GCS for example.

    2. Gimbal:

      A specialized camera mount which have the ability to rotate and tilt using servos or brushless motors. This allows the camera to stay in the same position, regardless of the drone’s movement, making for a very still and smooth looking image.

    3. GIT:

      A version control system for software developers.

    4. Gloabal Positioning System:

      Used to track movement or hold position at pre-defined coordinates.

    5. Ground Effect:

      The effect of added aerodynamic buoyancy produced by a cushion of air below a vehicle moving close to the ground.

    6. Gyroscope:

      Provides the angular velocity around 3 axes of space in degrees. Assists with keeping a quadcopter level and facing the same direction.
  • H

    1. Hexacopter:

      A type of multicopter, that has 6 motors, no servos, all motors on same level.

    2. Hobbyking:

      A famous RC Vendor based in China but with EU, US, AU, RU warehouses as well.
  • I

    1. Inertial Measurement Unit:

      A Break Out Board containing sensors that reads and sends data to the flight controller.
  • J

    1. JST-SH:

      A type of Connector.
  • K

    1. Kalman Filter:

      The algorithm used in Multicopter Flight controllers. It is primarily used to combine accelerometer and gyro data to provide an accurate description of aircraft attitude and movement in real time.
  • L

    1. LC FILTER:

      An LC Filer is a circuit that removes frequencies (noises) from a power supply and smooths / cleans it so the equipment being powered.

    2. Lithium Polymer Battery:

      Aka LiPoly. Most used power source for RC Hobby these days because of its high energy storage density-to-weight.

    3. Line of Sight:

      When you are flying your aircraft by watching your aircraft directly, you are flying LOS. The way of flying is FPV.

    4. Low-pass Filter:

      A technique that filters out high frequency signal.
  • M

    1. Magnetometer:

      An electronic compass – used to let our FC ( Flight Controller ) know which direction it is pointed in, compared to the Earth’s Magnetic field.

    2. MegaPirate:

      Aflight controller program software that copies Arducopter.

    3. MultiWii Copter:

      MultiWii is a general purpose software to control a multirotor RC model. It can use various sensors but was initially developed to support Nintendo Wii console gyroscopes and accelerometers.
  • N

    1. N/A
  • O

    1. Octocopter:

      A type of multicopter, that has 8 motors, no servos, all motors on same level.

    2. Oneshot125:

      A faster way of communication between FC and ESC.

    3. On Screen Display:

      A piece of hardware that overlays flight data in text or graphical form over an existing live stream video.
  • P

    1. Printed Circuit Board:

      A board with copper traces used to connect various electronic components such as ESCs, Video transmitter LEDs etc.

    2. Proportional Integral Derivative:

      The three parameters that you can adjust to change the copter’s flight performance. In theory PID is a control loop that attempts to minimize the difference between a measured process value and its desired value by adjusting the control input of a process.

    3. Pulse Position Modulation:

      A way of sending sequence of multiple signals down one signal wire.

    4. Pulse Width Modulation:

      The square-wave signals used in RC control to drive servos and speed controllers.
  • Q

    1. Quadcopter:

      A type of multicopter that has 4 motors, no servos, all motors on same level.
  • R

    1. Radio Control:

      The foundation of our hobby, it’s also used to refer to the hobby as a whole, radio controlled cars, multirotor, planes boats and more.

    2. RCGroups:

      A very popular RC forum

    3. Revolution:

      The 3rd generation of OpenPilot Controllers.

    4. Radio Frequency:

      Common frequency bands RC hobby use are 5.8gHz, 2.4gHz, 1.2gHz, 433mHz, 900mHz Not all bands are available in all countries, and some require a HAM (Amateur Radio Operator) license.

    5. Revolutions Per Minute:

      The number of times a motor shaft rotates a full cycle in 60 seconds.

    6. Received Signal Strength Indication:

      RSSI is a measurement of how good your radio signal is.

    7. Ready to Fly:

      Refers to the multicopter you buy has everything you need to start flying. You just open up the packaging, charge and install the batteries, and off you go!

    8. Return to Home:

      A GPS feature that when enabled, it returns the aircraft to the “home” position where it took off.

    9. Return To Launch:

      Same as RTH.

    10. Rudder:

      Also known as yaw.

    11. Radio Receiver:

      A device that receives commands from our radio Transmitter, and sends them directly to the servos or to the Flight Controller.
  • S

    1. SBUS:

      SBUS is a type of serial communication protocols, shared by Futaba and FrSky, that supports up to 18 channels using only one signal cable. Check out this post about different radio signal.

    2. Slow fly:

      Propellers that are designed to go on a motor using a gear reduction to lower prop speed.

    3. SimonK:

      A famous ESC firmware optomised for multirotors.

    4. Sub-Miniature Version A – Reverse Polarity SMA:

      Different Connectors for FPV gear.
  • T

    1. Throttle:

      Controls on your radio transmitter, it changes the speed of the motors.

    2. Tricopter:

      A type of multicopter that has 3 motors, one servo for yaw, all motors on same level.

    3. TV Line:

      TVL (TV Lines) is basically a measure of how good the FPV camera resolution is. The number is based on how many alternating black and white lines can be displayed in its image horizontally.

    4. Transmitter:

      A device that sends our commands to the Receiver, controlled by the pilot.
  • U

    1. Universal Asynchronous Receiver/Transmitter:

      Serial communication port. A piece of computer hardware that translates data between parallel and serial form.

    2. Unmanned Aerial Vehicle:

      Aircraft without a human pilot onboard. Control is provided by an onboard computer, remote control or combination of both.

    3. Universal Battery Elimination Circuit:

      A direct current voltage regulator.
  • V

    1. Video Receiver:

      A device that receives video signal from our video Transmitter, and display the live footage on our FPV goggle or screen.

    2. V-tail:

      A type of multicopter that has 4 motors, 2 tail motors form a V shape, has no servo.

    3. Video Transmitter:

      A device that sends video signal to our video receiver, video captured from a on-board camera.
  • W

    1. N/A
  • X

    1. X8:

      A type of multicopter that has 8 motors and of a shape of X, 4 motors on top, 4 motors at bottom, no servo.

    2. XT60:

      A type of connector.
  • Y

    1. Y4:

      A type of multicopter that has 4 motors, 2 tail motors one on top of ther other, it looks like a tricopter but has no servo.

    2. Y6:

      A type of multicopter that has 6 motors and of a shape of Y, 3 motors on top, 3 motors at bottom, no servo.

    3. Yaw:

      Flight term used to describe the rotation of a drone around it’s center axis. Controls which direction the quadcopter is facing.
  • Z

    1. N/A
